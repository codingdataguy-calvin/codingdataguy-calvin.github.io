Background

What I do &
how I work

I am a data quality and governance specialist with a track record of delivering high-impact engagements in complex, data-dense environments. My work sits at the intersection of data quality, governance, and business risk — helping organisations understand the true state of their data and build the structures to keep it healthy.

I have worked across telecommunications and financial services, with particular depth in subscriber data integrity, KYC compliance, core banking migrations, and enterprise-wide data stewardship. My engagements include M&A due diligence contexts where data findings have had direct board-level commercial consequences.

My career path is deliberate: from Data Analyst to Commercial Banking Officer to Data Steward II at one of Nigeria's tier-one banks. That progression means I understand what data represents in business terms — not just what it looks like in a database. I am focused on building a career in UK financial services data governance.

Subscriber Data Integrity
in an M&A Context

Telecoms Data Quality M&A Due Diligence Nigeria

A full-lifecycle data quality audit for a Nigerian telecoms operator undergoing ownership transition. Spanning ~21.8 million gross subscriber records, the engagement identified 1.8M duplicates, 4M inactive subscribers, and a ₦23.5M credit recovery risk — directly informing the acquisition valuation. KYC remediation improved collections from 9% to 30%.

29% reduction in reported subscriber base after deduplication and inactivity classification
600+ enterprise accounts misclassified — revenue leakage corrected across highest-value segment
9% to 30% collection improvement on ₦23.5M student loan book after KYC remediation

Embedded Data Stewardship
at GTCO Plc

Financial Services Data Stewardship Core Banking Migration Nigeria

26 months of embedded data stewardship at Guaranty Trust Bank Plc (GTCO Plc), culminating in a 90-day Basis-to-Finacle core banking migration. Identified a ₦4.2B NPL portfolio, improved NIN/BVN linkage from 9% to 33% in 60 days, and governed 3.9M customer records across 52 branches.

₦3.8B NPL write-offs enabled by migration classification work during CBN recapitalisation
33% NIN/BVN linkage achieved in 60 days, up from 9% over the prior 12 months
100,000+ duplicate accounts consolidated into single Customer Information Files
